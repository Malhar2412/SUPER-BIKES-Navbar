World's Most Powerful Superbikes

Explore our top picks for the best superbikes in the market.

Kawasaki Ninja H2R - 326 horsepower

The true king of superbikes is not a street-legal motorcycle.It has a 988cc liquid-cooled DOHC supercharged four-cylinder engine that can go 0-60 in 2.93 seconds and 0-100 in just 5.12 seconds. This makes it the fastest production motorcycle on the market.

Starting MSRP: $57,500
Ducati Panigale V4 R - 240.5 horsepower

Aprilia may be an exciting Italian brand, but let's face it, it's really hard to compete with Ducati when they're firing on all four cylinders. Which brings us to the Panigale V4 R, a sophisticated work of art that also happens to be a high-speed machine.

Starting MSRP: $44,995
Ducati Superleggera V4 - 234 horsepower

Ducati doesn't seem to know how to make a slow motorcycle. It's not in their DNA. Everything they make cranks it up to 11. And so is the case with this extremely exclusive Superleggera V4. Only 500 of them were ever produced, and each came with a certificate of authenticity.

Starting MSRP: $100,000
Kawasaki Ninja H2/H2 Carbon - 228 horsepower

Did we mention that Kawasaki has a lot of Ninjas in their stable? Carrying on the torch for the ZX-14 is the Kawasaki Ninja H2 and H2 Carbon as the flagship fastest bike in the world. Kawasaki makes each bike to order and requires a hefty deposit to ensure you have the right level of commitment.

Starting MSRP: $30,500/$34,000
Aprilia RSV4 1100 Factory - 217 horsepower

In case you weren't aware, but Ducati isn't the only Italian motorcycle manufacturer. They may get most of the mainstream attention, but true superbike enthusiasts know that Aprilia is one to pay attention to.

Starting MSRP: $25,999
Honda CBR 1000RR-R Fireblade SP - 215 horsepower

If you want a bike that embodies MotoGP technology but is also street-legal, then take a closer look at the Honda CBR 1000RR-R Fireblade SP. Its status, ever since its inception back in the early 90s, is legendary, to say the least.

Starting MSRP: $28,500
